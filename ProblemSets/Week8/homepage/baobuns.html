Hoisin Pork Baobuns - 4 people

Buns

  • 2 tsp. dry yeast
  • 2 dl milk, lukewarm
  • 5 dl wheat flour
  • 3 tbsp. granulated sugar
  • 0.5 tsp. salt
  • 3 pinches baking powder
  • 3 pinches baking soda
  • 2 tbsp. neutral cooking oil
  • 2 tbsp. sesame oil, for brushing

1. In a dough mixer, mix yeast and milk. Add the remaining ingredients and mix at the lowest speed for about 10 minutes. The dough should be smooth and shiny. Let it rise until double in size or for 1-1.5 hours under plastic wrap.

2. Place the dough on the work surface and divide it into 24 equal pieces. Let them rise for 30 minutes under a clean kitchen towel.

3. Roll out each piece of dough into a 10 cm oval shape. Brush lightly with sesame oil and then fold over one side to form a crescent shape.

3. If using a steam oven: Place parchment paper on a baking sheet and spread out all the dough pieces on the parchment paper. If using a bamboo basket: Cut out 24 10x10 cm pieces of parchment paper. Place a piece of dough on a piece of parchment paper and do the same with all the dough pieces.

4. Let them rise under the towel again for 45 minutes and then steam for 10 minutes either in a steam oven or a bamboo basket.

Filling/Toppings

  • 500 g sliced lightly cured pork belly without rind
  • 0.5 dl hoisin sauce, plus extra for serving
  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 clove of garlic, grated
  • 0.5 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 6 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1 dl peanuts, finely chopped
  • 1.5 dl mayonnaise
  • Sriracha

1. While the bread is rising, prepare the filling. Cut the sliced pork belly into bite-sized pieces. Mix hoisin sauce with sesame oil, ginger, and garlic. Fry the pork on high heat until golden. Then pour the flavored hoisin sauce over it and let it cook for another minute. Keep warm.

2. Once the bread is ready, fill them with pork, cucumber, and mayonnaise. Sprinkle chopped scallions, peanuts, and sriracha sauce on top. Serve immediately!
